  • Townland

    Two childhood friends Emily and Lucy meet up over Christmas break after beginning university in two different cities. They find that although their lives are rapidly changing their hometown has not, no matter how much Lucy might wish it would. As they talk it becomes clear that Emily is struggling to break free of their old life in the town despite her best efforts. The characters soon rediscover their unresolved issues but that their attitudes towards them could not be more different. Lucy want to run away from who she once was, Emily wants to take responsibility for their actions.

    TownLand is a new play set in the West of Ireland, between 2008 – 2018, exploring the changing face of rural Irish towns, the people who live there and the contemporary meaning of home.

  • The Minimum Requirement

    Determined and ambitious, twenty-four-year-old Abiola Okonkwo is en route to becoming a solicitor; an assistant at a top law firm in London, she’s finally saved enough money to start her training. That is until she learns she is below the newly introduced Minimum Pay Requirement, and has 30 days to leave the UK … or else she will be deported to Nigeria.

    The Minimum Pay Requirement follows Abiola’s efforts to appeal this harsh decision, and gain the right to remain in the UK. When she loses her appeal, Abiola turns her attention to more desperate measures: a fraudulent marriage with a British citizen. A serendipitous conversation on the morning of the wedding, however, makes her reconsider what is the minimum requirement for her happiness.

    The Minimum Requirement explores current immigration policy in the UK, friendship, and the lengths a person will go to attain a sense of belonging.
