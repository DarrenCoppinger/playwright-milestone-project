About

Neasa O’Callaghan is a playwright and producer from Co. Roscommon, currently based in Dublin. She holds a BA in English, Classics and Creative Writing from NUI Galway, an MPhil in Irish Writing from Trinity College, Dublin, and an MRes in Playwriting Studies from the University of Birmingham. In 2015-16, she was a Resident Writer at Theatre503 in London, a Writer-on-Attachment at the Oxford Playhouse, and a Writer-in-Residence at the North Wall Arts Centre, Oxford. In 2017-18, she was one of 30 playwrights chosen for Fishamble: The New Play Company’s ‘A Play for Ireland’ Scheme.

As a producer, she completed the Smock Generator Creative Producer Training Programme at Smock Alley Theatre in 2016, and the Irish Theatre Institute / Town Hall Theatre ELEVATOR Producer Mentoring Programme in 2017. She has produced work at the Dublin Fringe Festival, for JOLT Theatre, at Dublin Theatre Festival among others. In 2018-19, she worked at the Gate Theatre, Dublin, as Marketing and Theatre Administrator, where she assisted with the company’s marketing campaigns and communications strategy. She is currently the Marketing & Development Manager at Dublin Fringe Festival, Ireland’s largest multi-disciplinary arts festival.
